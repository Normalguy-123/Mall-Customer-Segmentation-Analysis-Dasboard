# Mall-Customer-Segmentation-Analysis-Dasboard

# Project Overview

This project analyzes customer data to understand spending behavior across different customer groups. The goal is to identify high, medium, and low spenders, examine customer distribution by gender, and evaluate how age and income influence spending score. The analysis helps businesses make better marketing and customer retention decisions.

# Objectives

The main objectives of this project are to:

* segment customers based on spending behavior
* examine customer distribution by gender
* identify the age groups with the highest average spending score
* analyze spending score across income groups
* provide actionable insights and recommendations for business growth

# Dataset Information

The dataset contains 200 customers and includes the following variables:

* Customer ID
* Gender
* Age
* Annual Income
* Spending Score
* Customer Segment
# Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

* Total Customers: 200
*  Average Age: 38.9 years
*  Average Annual Income: 60.6
  
#  Customer Segmentation by Spending Behavior:

  * High Spenders: 29.88%
  * Medium Spenders: 37.60%
  * Low Spenders: 32.52%
    
*  Customer Distribution by Gender:**

  * Male: 54.36%
  * Female: 45.64%

# Business Questions

This analysis answers the following questions:

1. What percentage of customers belong to high, medium, and low spending groups?
2. What is the customer distribution by gender?
3. Which age groups have the highest average spending score?
4. How does spending behavior differ across income groups?
5. What business opportunities can be identified from customer spending patterns?

# Key Insights

* Medium spenders represent the largest customer segment at 37.60%, showing an opportunity to convert them into high spenders.
* Low spenders account for 32.52%, while high spenders make up 29.88% of customers.
* Male customers represent 54.36% of the customer base, while female customers account for 45.64%.
* Customers aged 28–37 have the highest average spending score of 61.6, followed by the 18–27 age group with 55.6.
* Spending tends to decline among older age groups, especially customers aged 58–67, who recorded the lowest average spending score of 36.0.
* The income group 93–105 has the highest average spending score of 53.4, showing that spending behavior is not limited only to the highest income earners.

# Recommendations

* Develop targeted promotions for medium spenders to encourage them to become high-value customers.
* Focus marketing campaigns on customers aged 18–37, since they show the strongest spending behavior.
* Create loyalty programs and discount offers for low spenders to increase purchase frequency.
* Design personalized campaigns for high-performing income groups to maximize customer value.
* Maintain balanced product and marketing strategies for both male and female customers.
